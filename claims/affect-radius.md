# Claim: Affect Radius Expands With Material Security

## Statement

The range of entities a human is capable of caring about — their affect radius — is directly correlated with material security. Insecurity contracts the radius inward (self, then family). Security expands it outward (community, society, humanity). This is not a moral choice. It is a cognitive allocation governed by the same threat-processing that drives the poverty-cognition link.

## Mechanism

Under threat, cognitive resources are allocated to immediate survival and protection of the closest bonds. Empathy, abstract concern for strangers, and systemic thinking require executive function bandwidth that threat-processing pre-empts. As material security increases, the threat-processing load decreases, freeing bandwidth for wider circles of concern.

This creates a self-reinforcing cycle in both directions:
- **Downward spiral:** Insecurity → contracted radius → political indifference to others' floors → floors stay low → more insecurity
- **Upward spiral:** Security → expanded radius → political will to raise floors → floors rise → more security

## Key Evidence

- **Maslow's hierarchy (1943, 1954):** The original observation that higher-order concerns (belonging, esteem, self-actualization) emerge only after lower-order needs (physiological, safety) are met. Widely replicated despite debates about strict hierarchy.
- **Inglehart's post-materialist values thesis (1977, 1997):** Societies that achieve material security undergo a measurable shift from "survival values" (economic and physical security) to "self-expression values" (tolerance, environmentalism, concern for others). Documented across 100+ countries in the World Values Survey spanning decades.
- **Prosocial behavior and socioeconomic security:** Piff et al. (2010) and subsequent work show complex relationships between wealth and prosocial behavior, but the consistent finding is that *perceived* security (not absolute wealth) predicts generosity and concern for others.
- **Stress and empathy:** Chronic stress reduces empathic accuracy (Tomova et al., 2014). The mechanism is consistent — threat-processing competes with empathy for the same cognitive resources.
- **Critical nuance — extreme wealth contraction:** Very high wealth can paradoxically contract the radius when maintaining position becomes its own survival project. The relationship is not linear but follows an inverted-U: maximum radius occurs at secure-but-not-positionally-threatened.

## Implication for the Framework

The affect radius dynamic explains why floor-raising is self-reinforcing once it begins. Each person whose floor is raised becomes a person whose radius expands, which creates more political support for raising more floors. It also explains why the current low-floor environment resists change — contracted radii produce electorates that cannot prioritize collective solutions.

## Status

**Evidenced.** The core correlation between material security and expanded concern is among the most robust findings in cross-cultural psychology. The Inglehart data alone spans decades and 100+ societies. The causal mechanism (threat-processing pre-empting empathy) is supported by neuroscience.
