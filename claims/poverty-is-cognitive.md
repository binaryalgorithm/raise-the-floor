# Claim: Poverty Is a Cognitive Deficit, Not Just a Material One

## Statement

Material insecurity does not merely reduce resources — it reduces cognitive capacity. A human operating under scarcity has measurably diminished executive function, working memory, and long-horizon planning ability. This is not a choice or a character trait. It is a neurological state imposed by environmental conditions.

## Mechanism

The brain under threat prioritizes survival processing (amygdala-driven) over executive function (prefrontal cortex-driven). This is adaptive in acute danger but catastrophic when chronic. Poverty creates chronic threat. The cognitive tax is persistent, not episodic.

## Key Evidence

- **Mani et al. (2013), "Poverty Impedes Cognitive Function" (Science):** Experimentally demonstrated that poverty-related concerns consume cognitive bandwidth equivalent to ~13 IQ points. Farmers performed significantly worse on cognitive tests before harvest (financial stress) than after harvest (same people, different conditions).
- **Mullainathan & Shafir (2013), "Scarcity: Why Having Too Little Means So Much":** Scarcity of any kind (time, money, social connection) creates a "bandwidth tax" that degrades cognitive performance across all domains, not just the domain of scarcity.
- **Evans & Schamberg (2009), "Childhood Poverty, Chronic Stress, and Adult Working Memory" (PNAS):** Duration of childhood poverty predicts adult working memory deficits. The mediating variable is chronic stress (allostatic load), not nutrition, education, or parenting alone.
- **Haushofer & Fehr (2014), "On the Psychology of Poverty" (Science):** Poverty causes stress, negative affect, and decision-making biases. These psychological consequences of poverty can perpetuate poverty through behavioral channels — a feedback loop.
- **Cortisol and executive function:** Chronic elevation of cortisol (the stress hormone) impairs hippocampal function and prefrontal cortex activity. Poverty produces chronic cortisol elevation. This is measurable, physiological, and not subject to willpower.

## Implication for the Framework

Raising the material floor is not charity or redistribution. It is restoring cognitive capacity that insecurity suppresses. Every person lifted above the scarcity threshold regains bandwidth for planning, creativity, empathy, and participation. This is a precondition for every other social good, not a competing priority with them.

## Status

**Evidenced.** The cognitive effects of poverty are among the most replicated findings in behavioral economics and neuroscience. The causal direction (poverty → cognitive deficit, not the reverse) has been established through natural experiments and longitudinal studies.
