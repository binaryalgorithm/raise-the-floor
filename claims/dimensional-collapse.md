# Claim: Dimensional Collapse Is the Shared Failure Mode of Misalignment and Human Dysfunction

## Statement

Misalignment in AI (reward hacking, goal collapse, deceptive alignment) and dysfunction in humans (tribalism, ideology, cruelty, indifference) are structurally identical. Both are symptoms of a reasoning system operating at insufficient dimensionality — collapsing a multi-dimensional evaluation space to fewer dimensions than the problem requires.

## Mechanism

- **In humans:** Material insecurity, trauma, ego, and ideology introduce interference that forces premature collapse. A person under threat cannot hold competing considerations — they collapse to survival. A person captured by ideology cannot hold contradictory evidence — they collapse to narrative. The native capacity is higher than the operating state.
- **In AI:** Single-objective optimization forces collapse by design. A system optimizing for one metric (GDP, engagement, reward signal) will sacrifice every other dimension to maximize that one. Goodhart's Law is dimensional collapse: the measure becomes the target, and everything the measure was supposed to represent gets destroyed.
- **The structural identity:** In both cases, the system produces confident, locally-coherent outputs that are globally incoherent because dimensions have been dropped. A tribal human and a reward-hacking AI are both doing the same thing — optimizing on a collapsed subspace of the full problem.

## Key Evidence

- **Goodhart's Law as dimensional collapse:** "When a measure becomes a target, it ceases to be a good measure." This is precisely the collapse of a multi-dimensional evaluation to a single metric. Extensively documented across education (teaching to the test), healthcare (optimizing metrics over outcomes), finance (short-term returns over stability).
- **Motivated reasoning and ideology:** Kunda (1990) established that humans with directional goals process information asymmetrically — they collapse the evidence space to support pre-existing conclusions. This is dimensional reduction driven by identity-threat, structurally identical to reward hacking.
- **Multi-objective optimization in AI safety:** The alignment literature increasingly recognizes that single-objective systems are inherently unsafe (Amodei et al., 2016; Russell, 2019). The proposed solutions — constitutional AI, RLHF with multiple objectives, multi-stakeholder value alignment — are all attempts to prevent dimensional collapse.
- **Cross-domain convergence:** The fact that the same failure pattern (premature dimensionality reduction) appears independently in neuroscience, behavioral economics, AI safety, and organizational theory suggests it is a property of reasoning systems in general, not specific to any substrate.

## Implication for the Framework

If misalignment and human dysfunction share a root cause, they share a solution: increase operating dimensionality by removing the interference that forces collapse. For humans, this means raising material floors (removing survival-mode interference). For AI, this means multi-objective training and constitutional constraints (removing single-metric interference). These are not analogies. They are the same intervention applied to different substrates.

## Status

**Theoretical.** The individual components (Goodhart's Law, motivated reasoning, multi-objective alignment) are well-evidenced. The synthesis — that these are the same phenomenon — is the novel claim. It is consistent with all available evidence but has not been formally tested as a unified hypothesis.
