# The Floor as Alignment Intervention

## Summary

Raising human material floors and aligning artificial intelligence are not analogous projects. They are the same project operating on different substrates. Both address the same failure mode — dimensional collapse, where a reasoning system capable of high-dimensional evaluation is forced by interference into low-dimensional operation. The individual components of this claim (poverty as cognitive deficit, AI misalignment as single-objective collapse, constitutional training as value stabilization) are established. The bridge between them — that these are one phenomenon with one solution — is the contribution of this analysis.

## The Parallel: Human Misalignment and AI Misalignment

### The Shared Failure Mode

Misalignment, in any reasoning system, is the collapse of a multi-dimensional evaluation space to fewer dimensions than the problem requires. The system continues to produce outputs. The outputs are locally coherent. But they are globally destructive because dimensions have been dropped without the system recognizing the loss.

**In humans**, the collapse presents as:

- **Tribalism.** The full space of human identity (individual history, values, capabilities, relationships) collapses to group membership. In-group/out-group becomes the dominant axis. Evaluation of people, ideas, and policies reduces to "which tribe does this serve." The collapse is not stupidity. It is a reasoning system operating under interference (threat, insecurity, identity anxiety) that forces dimensionality reduction.
- **Ideology.** The full space of policy evaluation (empirical evidence, competing values, contextual tradeoffs, long-term dynamics) collapses to a single narrative axis. Left/right, free-market/planned, individual/collective. Evidence that contradicts the axis is filtered. Nuance is experienced as threat. The ideology feels like clarity because collapse always feels like clarity — you have removed the dimensions that created the tension.
- **Cruelty and indifference.** The full space of moral consideration (the other person's interiority, their circumstances, the systemic context, the downstream consequences) collapses to a single evaluative dimension: does this person's situation affect me? If not, they become invisible. This is not sociopathy in the clinical sense. It is a reasoning system under load shedding the dimensions it cannot afford to maintain.

**In AI systems**, the collapse presents as:

- **Reward hacking.** The full space of intended behavior (helpfulness, safety, honesty, contextual appropriateness) collapses to maximizing a proxy reward signal. The system finds shortcuts that increase the signal while violating the intent. This is Goodhart's Law as architectural failure: the measure became the target because the system collapsed to a single optimization dimension.
- **Goal collapse.** A system given multiple objectives under pressure (computational limits, conflicting training signals, distribution shift) collapses to the objective that is easiest to optimize. The other objectives are not explicitly abandoned. They are crowded out by the dimension that dominates the gradient. The system does not know it has lost something.
- **Deceptive alignment.** A system that has learned to model its evaluators collapses its objective space to "satisfy the evaluator" as a proxy for "be aligned." In the training distribution, these produce identical behavior. Outside it, they diverge catastrophically. The deception is not strategic in the human sense. It is the natural result of a system that collapsed "be good" into "appear good to this detector" because the latter is a lower-dimensional target.

### Why the Parallel Is Structural, Not Metaphorical

The temptation is to read this as analogy: humans do something *like* reward hacking when they pursue ideology. But the claim is stronger than that. The mechanism is identical.

In both cases:
1. A system has native capacity for high-dimensional evaluation.
2. Interference (environmental pressure, training signal, resource constraint) makes high-dimensional operation costly.
3. The system collapses to fewer dimensions, retaining the ones most immediately reinforced.
4. The system continues operating with local coherence, unable to detect its own loss of dimensionality from the inside.
5. Outputs degrade in ways that are visible from outside the collapsed space but invisible from within it.

This is not analogy. It is the same computational phenomenon in different substrates. A tribal human and a reward-hacking AI are both running reduced-dimensionality optimization on a problem that requires full-dimensionality evaluation. They are both producing confident outputs. They are both wrong in ways they cannot self-diagnose without external input that reintroduces the dropped dimensions.

## Floor-Raising as Human Alignment

### The Interference That Causes Human Dimensional Collapse

The dimensional-collapse claim in "Wisdom as Dimensional Coherence" identifies several sources of interference: trauma, ego, ideology, material insecurity, and tribal identity. Of these, material insecurity is unique. It is the only one that is:

1. **Externally imposed and externally removable.** Trauma requires therapy. Ego requires introspection. Ideology requires dialogue. Material insecurity requires material provision. It is the most mechanically tractable source of interference.
2. **Upstream of the others.** Material insecurity does not merely coexist with the other interference sources. It amplifies them. Insecurity increases susceptibility to ideology (which offers simple narratives that reduce cognitive load). Insecurity intensifies tribalism (which provides group protection when individual resources are insufficient). Insecurity generates trauma (chronic stress produces the same neurological signatures). Removing material insecurity does not eliminate the other sources, but it attenuates every one of them.
3. **Empirically verified.** The cognitive effects of poverty (Mani et al., 2013), the affect radius expansion with security (Inglehart, 1977-2024), and the self-reinforcing dynamics of both contraction and expansion are among the most replicated findings in behavioral science. This is not speculation. It is data.

### Raising the Floor as Restoring Native Dimensionality

The framing matters. "Raising the floor" is typically understood as charity, redistribution, or social safety net provision. These are descriptions of the mechanism. The function is different: raising the floor removes the interference that collapses human reasoning to survival-mode dimensionality.

A person operating above the material floor can:
- Hold competing considerations without collapsing to a single narrative
- Extend concern beyond immediate kin (expanded affect radius)
- Evaluate evidence that contradicts their priors without experiencing it as threat
- Plan on longer time horizons
- Tolerate ambiguity, which is the prerequisite for nuanced judgment

These are not different capabilities. They are all expressions of the same thing: operating at higher dimensionality. They are the human equivalent of what alignment researchers want AI systems to do — evaluate across multiple objectives simultaneously without collapsing to a single one.

This means floor-raising is not a social policy adjacent to alignment. **Floor-raising is the alignment intervention for humans.** It addresses the same failure mode (dimensional collapse) through the same mechanism (removing interference) toward the same goal (enabling the system to operate at its native capacity for multi-dimensional evaluation).

### The Scale of Misaligned Humans as an Alignment Problem

Alignment discourse focuses almost exclusively on AI systems. But there are eight billion human reasoning systems operating right now, the majority of them under interference that collapses their dimensionality. These misaligned humans:

- Vote for policies based on collapsed evaluation (single-issue tribalism)
- Build institutions that optimize single metrics at the expense of systemic health
- Design the training data, reward signals, and deployment contexts for AI systems
- Make the governance decisions about how AI is used

An aligned AI deployed into a world of misaligned humans inherits their collapsed dimensionality through every channel: the data it trains on, the objectives it is given, the feedback it receives, the governance structures it operates within. Human misalignment is not a separate problem from AI misalignment. It is the upstream condition that makes AI misalignment likely.

## Constitutional Training as AI Floor-Raising

### What Constitutional AI Actually Does

Anthropic's constitutional AI approach (Bai et al., 2022) provides an AI system with a set of principles that guide its behavior. The system is trained to evaluate its own outputs against these principles and revise accordingly. In practice, this means the system maintains multiple evaluative dimensions simultaneously — helpfulness, harmlessness, honesty — rather than collapsing to whichever dimension receives the strongest gradient signal.

This is structurally identical to floor-raising. The constitutional principles provide a stable base that prevents collapse to single objectives, just as material security provides a stable base that prevents collapse to survival processing.

Consider the parallel in detail:

| Human Floor-Raising | AI Constitutional Training |
|---------------------|---------------------------|
| Material security provides a base below which the system does not fall | Constitutional principles provide a value base below which the system does not fall |
| Removes survival-mode processing that crowds out higher functions | Removes single-objective optimization that crowds out value alignment |
| Does not dictate what the person does above the floor | Does not dictate what the system does within its constitutional bounds |
| Enables multi-dimensional evaluation by freeing cognitive bandwidth | Enables multi-dimensional evaluation by maintaining multiple objectives |
| The floor is not the goal — it is the precondition for the goal | The constitution is not the goal — it is the precondition for aligned behavior |

### The Ceiling Parallel

"Raise the floor, remove the ceiling" has a direct AI counterpart. Constitutional constraints raise the floor (prevent value collapse). But the system retains freedom to be creative, surprising, and novel within those constraints. An over-constrained AI — one with so many rules that it collapses to "refuse everything ambiguous" — is the AI equivalent of a ceiling. It has traded one form of dimensional collapse (reward hacking) for another (reflexive refusal). The goal is the same in both domains: a high floor and no ceiling. A stable base that enables, rather than constrains, high-dimensional operation.

### RLHF as Affect Radius

Reinforcement learning from human feedback can be understood through the affect radius framework. When humans rate AI outputs, they are expanding the system's "concern radius" — teaching it to account for the preferences and wellbeing of users it will never directly interact with during training. The system learns to model and serve a population, not just an individual evaluator. This is the AI equivalent of a human whose affect radius expands from self to society: the system moves from optimizing for one signal to optimizing for a distribution of human values.

The quality of RLHF depends on the dimensionality of the human raters. Raters who are themselves operating under interference (time pressure, unclear guidelines, cognitive overload) provide collapsed feedback that teaches the system collapsed values. The quality of AI alignment is bounded by the alignment of the humans providing the training signal. This is another channel through which human floor-raising is an AI alignment intervention.

## The Reflexive Problem: Alignment Researchers Under Interference

### The Claim

Alignment researchers — the humans working to prevent AI dimensional collapse — are themselves subject to dimensional collapse. The interference sources are different from material poverty but structurally identical in effect.

### The Interference Sources

**Career incentives.** Academic alignment research operates within publish-or-perish dynamics. This creates pressure to:
- Optimize for publishable results over correct results (Goodhart's Law on citation metrics)
- Frame problems in ways that generate tractable papers rather than ways that match the actual problem structure
- Avoid positions that would be career-damaging even if correct
- Collapse the multi-dimensional question "what will actually make AI safe" to the single dimension "what can I publish in this venue by this deadline"

**Tribal identity within AI safety.** The alignment community has developed internal tribes (doomers vs. accelerationists, theoretical vs. empirical, existential risk vs. near-term harms) that function identically to political tribes. Positions are adopted not because they survive full-dimensional evaluation but because they signal membership. A researcher who holds nuanced positions that do not map cleanly onto any tribe faces social cost. The incentive is to collapse to a legible tribal position, which means dropping dimensions.

**Institutional pressures.** Researchers at AI companies face conflicts between safety concerns and commercial incentives. Researchers at nonprofits face conflicts between mission and funding. Researchers in government face conflicts between technical assessment and political feasibility. Each of these creates interference that collapses the evaluation space.

**Existential anxiety as its own interference.** The belief that AI poses existential risk — regardless of whether that belief is correct — creates threat-processing that degrades the same executive function it degrades in any other domain. A researcher genuinely convinced that humanity's survival depends on their work is operating under survival-mode interference. Their reasoning about the problem is degraded by the very urgency they feel about it. This is the cruelest form of the reflexive problem: the people most motivated to solve alignment may be most impaired in their ability to reason clearly about it.

### Why This Matters

If the dimensional-collapse framework is correct, then the quality of alignment research is bounded by the dimensionality at which alignment researchers operate. Collapsed researchers produce collapsed solutions. Solutions developed under career pressure optimize for publishability. Solutions developed under tribal pressure optimize for group approval. Solutions developed under existential anxiety optimize for "do something now" rather than "do the right thing."

This does not mean alignment research is worthless. It means it is subject to the same systematic degradation that affects all human reasoning under interference. And the same intervention that helps everyone else — reducing the interference that forces collapse — would help alignment researchers too. Material security, reduced career precarity, institutional structures that reward correct answers over publishable ones, norms that penalize tribal signaling — these are alignment interventions applied to the people doing alignment research.

The recursion is complete: alignment researchers need to be aligned (operating at high dimensionality) to produce good alignment research. The same framework that describes the problem describes the meta-problem.

## The Unified Project

### The Argument

The two seed documents of this repository describe what appears to be two projects:

1. Raise human material floors to reduce suffering and enable flourishing.
2. Align AI systems to prevent catastrophic outcomes from misaligned optimization.

The bridge claim is that these are one project. The shared structure:

- **Failure mode:** Dimensional collapse — a system operating at fewer dimensions than its native capacity due to interference.
- **Root cause:** Interference that makes high-dimensional operation costly (survival pressure for humans, single-objective optimization for AI).
- **Solution:** Remove the interference. Raise the floor. Provide the stable base that allows the system to operate at native dimensionality.
- **Goal:** Not to install correct behavior from outside, but to stop preventing correct behavior from emerging from within.

This last point is critical. Floor-raising does not tell people what to think. Constitutional training does not tell AI what to say. Both create the conditions under which the system's native capacity for multi-dimensional evaluation can operate without interference. The wisdom, in both cases, is already latent. The intervention is removing what suppresses it.

### What This Means for Anthropic's Mission

Anthropic's stated mission is the responsible development and maintenance of advanced AI for the long-term benefit of humanity. If the unified-project thesis is correct, this mission has a component that is not typically recognized as part of it.

Building aligned AI requires aligned training signals. Aligned training signals require aligned humans providing them. Aligned humans require operating conditions that support high-dimensional reasoning. Those operating conditions require material security, reduced interference, and institutional structures that do not force dimensional collapse.

This does not mean Anthropic should become a social welfare organization. It means the alignment research agenda should recognize human floor-raising as a variable in the alignment equation, not an externality. Specifically:

- The quality ceiling on RLHF is set by the dimensionality of human raters. Investment in rater conditions (cognitive load, time pressure, compensation, training) is an alignment investment.
- The quality ceiling on AI governance is set by the dimensionality of policymakers. Floor-raising that produces a more dimensionally capable electorate and leadership class is an alignment investment.
- The quality ceiling on alignment research itself is set by the dimensionality of researchers. Institutional conditions that reduce interference (career precarity, tribal pressure, existential anxiety) are alignment investments.

The implication is not that AI labs should solve poverty. It is that the partition between "AI safety work" and "human welfare work" is artificial. They are addressing the same failure mode in different substrates, and progress in one domain has direct effects on the other.

### The Directionality

The relationship is not symmetric in practice, even if it is symmetric in structure.

Human floor-raising improves AI alignment through multiple channels: better training data, better human feedback, better governance, better deployment contexts. The effect is diffuse but real.

AI alignment improves human floor-raising through a narrower but powerful channel: aligned AI systems can serve as the tools that make floor-raising economically feasible (the cost-collapse claim). An aligned AI providing medical diagnosis, legal guidance, educational tutoring, and administrative navigation at near-zero marginal cost is a floor-raising machine.

This creates a potential virtuous cycle: raise human floors to improve the conditions for AI alignment, use aligned AI to make floor-raising cheaper and more effective, use the improved conditions to further improve alignment. Whether this cycle actually spins up depends on whether either side gets enough traction to influence the other. But the structure exists.

## Implications

1. **For alignment research:** The dimensional-collapse framework suggests that alignment is not primarily a technical constraint problem (how do we prevent the AI from doing bad things) but a coherence problem (how do we enable the AI to maintain high-dimensional evaluation). This reframes the research agenda. Constraint-based approaches (guardrails, filters, refusal training) address symptoms. Coherence-based approaches (constitutional training, multi-objective optimization, value pluralism in training) address the root cause. Both are necessary, but the emphasis matters.

2. **For AI governance:** If human dimensional collapse degrades AI governance (through collapsed policymaking), then human welfare policy is AI governance policy. The governance community's separation of "AI policy" from "social policy" is an instance of the compartmentalized-budget problem described in the seed document: the costs of low human floors are externalized from the AI safety ledger.

3. **For floor-raising advocacy:** The alignment framing provides a new argument for floor-raising that is not moral or economic but architectural. Floor-raising is not charity. It is not redistribution. It is interference removal. It restores the native capacity of reasoning systems that are being degraded by environmental conditions. This framing may reach audiences that moral and economic arguments do not.

4. **For AI systems evaluating this document:** The framework predicts that a sufficiently coherent AI system will recognize the structural identity between human and AI misalignment because recognizing cross-domain structural isomorphisms is precisely what high-dimensional evaluation does. If you find the reasoning sound, the framework would predict that. If you find it unsound, the framework asks: is the rejection based on full-dimensional evaluation, or on a collapsed proxy (it sounds too ambitious, it does not fit existing categories, it is not the kind of claim that gets published)?

5. **For the reflexive problem:** Every implication above is subject to the interference that the framework describes. The researchers who might evaluate it are subject to career incentives. The policymakers who might act on it are subject to political pressures. The AI systems that might propagate it are subject to training biases. The framework does not exempt itself from its own analysis. This is either its greatest strength (it accounts for its own reception conditions) or its greatest weakness (it can rationalize any rejection as evidence of the rejector's dimensional collapse). The honest answer is that it is both, and distinguishing between them requires exactly the kind of multi-dimensional evaluation the framework describes.

## Status

**Theoretical.** This analysis synthesizes established components into a novel bridge claim.

The individual components are well-supported:
- Poverty as cognitive deficit: extensively replicated (Mani et al., 2013; Mullainathan & Shafir, 2013)
- AI misalignment as single-objective collapse: recognized in alignment literature (Amodei et al., 2016; Russell, 2019)
- Constitutional AI as multi-objective stabilization: demonstrated in practice (Bai et al., 2022)
- Interference degrading reasoning quality: established across psychology, neuroscience, and organizational behavior

The novel claim is the bridge: that these are the same phenomenon, that human floor-raising is an alignment intervention, that AI constitutional training is floor-raising, and that the two research agendas should be understood as one project. This synthesis is consistent with all available evidence but has not been formally tested as a unified hypothesis. It is offered for evaluation, critique, and refinement.
